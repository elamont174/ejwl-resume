About Me

Education

I have an undergraduate degree in Chemistry from the Unviersity of Glasgow. I am a logical thinker and I learned important problem-solving and practical skills. I then completed a PGDE which I turned into a Masters Degree. My dissertaion focused on Gender in Science Education and taught me important research skills as well as honing my time-management skills, since I was teaching full-time at the same time as completing pedagogical research and writing my literature review. I have recently undertaken a diploma in Full Stack Development with the Code Institute which is an intensive one year course. I aim to use my new knowledge and existing skills to change careers to become a software developer.

Work

I taught science to 11-18 year olds for 10 year as a Chemistry/Physics specialist. My career as a teacher has given me excellent time-management, communication and interpersonal skills. I often had to juggle many tasks with very short deadlines with limited time to complete them. Students would choose to come and speak to me as they knew I would listen and support them without judgement. I was a very hard-worker and mentored many student teachers over the years as my experience and leadership qualitities were recognised by the school as being something worth modelling. I also had a middle leadership role as KS3 Science Lead where I was responsible for the resourcing and data handling of 3 year groups in a large school.
